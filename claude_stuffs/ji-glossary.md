# a TINY just intonation glossary

Domain terminology for the MUICapstone project. Use these definitions when reasoning about tuning systems, reviewing just intonation logic, or writing code that handles frequency ratios.

## Terms

**just intonation** A tuning system that builds intervals from pure frequency ratios derived from the harmonic series, rather than dividing the octave into equal steps.

**extended just intonation** Just intonation using prime factors beyond the classical 2, 3, and 5. Incorporates primes 7, 11, 13, 17, 19, and higher, each opening a new dimension of harmonic space with no equivalent in twelve tone equal temperament.

**twelve tone equal temperament** The standard Western tuning system dividing the octave into twelve equally spaced semitones. Every interval except the octave is slightly out of tune relative to pure ratios.

**harmonic series** The series of overtones produced by a vibrating object at integer multiples of its fundamental frequency. The organizing principle for just intonation intervals and for the visual mapping in this system.

**prime limit** The largest prime factor in the ratio defining an interval. A 5-limit interval uses only primes 2, 3, and 5. A 7-limit interval introduces prime 7. Higher prime limits open more dimensions of harmonic space.

**beating frequency** The audible amplitude fluctuation produced when two tones are close but not identical in frequency. Pure just intonation intervals have zero beating. Tempered intervals always have some.

**combination tones** Additional tones produced at the sum and difference frequencies when two tones sound simultaneously. In just intonation, these fall on predictable harmonic series members. In equal temperament, they fall on irrational frequencies.

**spectral centroid** The weighted mean of the frequency spectrum. A measure of brightness. Higher values mean more energy in high frequencies.

**spectral flux** The difference between successive spectral frames. Measures how quickly the timbre is changing.

**mel frequency cepstral coefficients** A compact representation of the spectral envelope capturing timbral quality somewhat independent of pitch and loudness.

**cents** A logarithmic unit of measure for musical intervals. One octave equals 1200 cents. Used to express the deviation between just and tempered intervals.

**harmonic seventh** The interval 7/4, approximately 969 cents. 31 cents flatter than the equal tempered minor seventh (1000 cents) with a completely different spectral character.

**undecimal tritone** The interval 11/8, sitting between a perfect fourth and a tritone in a place that equal temperament cannot reach.

**latent space** The high-dimensional vector space that a generative adversarial network uses internally to represent images. The mapping layer translates audio descriptors into positions in this space.

## Reference Artists and Works

These artists and works represent the musical and theoretical lineage this project draws from. Use them as context when discussing the project's aesthetic and technical goals.

1. **Ben Johnston** *Ten String Quartets*. Extended just intonation composition exploring harmonic space up to high prime limits through the string quartet medium.
2. **Toby Twining** *Eurydice*. Vocal music exploring pure intonation and the acoustic phenomena that emerge from precisely tuned ensemble singing.
3. **Toby Twining** *Chrysalid Requiem*. Large-scale choral work built on just intonation principles.
4. **La Monte Young** *The Well-Tuned Piano*. Landmark installation demonstrating that sustained just intonation intervals produce acoustic phenomena that equal temperament cannot.
5. **Thomas Nicholson** YouTube. Microtonal and just intonation content.
6. **Acreil** YouTube. Microtonal and just intonation content.
